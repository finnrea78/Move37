Philosophy
==========

Penrose-Lamarck is more than a software exercise. The name reflects a
view about learning, reasoning, and human agency in the age of AI.

Purpose
-------

Penrose-Lamarck is a learning platform
for scientists and engineers who believe human understanding is more than
computation. In an era when models can generate text, code, and biological
sequences in milliseconds, we choose study not to become faster databases—but to
become deeper thinkers.

For this reason, exercise asks candidates not only to make code
changes, but more importantly to show how they reasoned, what they questioned, and how they
supervised the coding assistant they used.

Foundations
-----------

**The Penrose Defiance**
   .. image:: _static/authors/rogerpenrose.png
      :alt: Sir Roger Penrose
      :class: author-inline
      :align: right
      :width: 160px

   Roger Penrose argues that human understanding is not exhausted by formal
   computation alone. Whether or not one accepts that argument literally, it
   captures a useful intuition for this project: meaningful engineering work is
   not just the generation of correct-looking output, but the exercise of
   judgment about what is true, useful, and worth building.

**The Lamarckian Leap**
   .. image:: _static/authors/jeanbaptistedelamarck.jpg
      :alt: Jean-Baptiste Lamarck
      :class: author-inline
      :align: right
      :height: 160px

   Lamarck is invoked here as a metaphor for intentional intellectual
   development (organisms acquire traits through "use and disuse"). Engineers do
   not simply accumulate tools and facts by accident
   (Darwinian evolution happens over time through random mutation);
   they reshape their capabilities through repeated practice, deliberate effort,
   and exposure to demanding problems.

**Beyond Pure Computation**
   .. image:: _static/authors/stephenwolfram.png
      :alt: Stephen Wolfram
      :class: author-inline
      :align: right
      :width: 160px

   Digital physicists like Stephen Wolfram suggest that reality is computation,
   and by that token, we are all turing machines. But Penrose-Lamarck argues that
   AI is an enabler, not a model or a replacer for human ingenuity.

Why This Matters For The Exercise
---------------------------------

This philosophy is the reason the hiring exercise asks for:

- prompt history
- validation evidence
- notes on assistant mistakes and corrections
- honest reporting of incomplete work and trade-offs

The goal is not to let candidates let agents do their work for them,
the goal is to assess how candidates use agents to enhance themselves. AI does
not replace the human scientific enquiry; it liberates it. Penrose-Lamarck helps
internalize the deep knowledge that turns tool operators into authors of
breakthroughs:

    "We are what we repeatedly do. Excellence, then, is not an act, but a habit."
